• about us

    With a passion for helping those less fortunate than us, we started our journey in 2017 with the help of just a few friends and supporters. We started off with a basic aim, to make sure that the surplus food in hotels, restaurants and function halls don’t go in the trash, rather be fed to the needy. Over the span of 3 years, this sporadic activity became a full-time nation-wide organization beyond just feeding leftovers to the needy.

OUR WORK

ANAJ BANK

This initiative was started by Bait ul Imdaad charitable Trust in May 2017. The main purpose was to make good use of surplus food at hotels, restaurants,function halls and houses and serve the same to the poor and hungry to ensure that no person goes hungry in the city. Food is distributed in Orphanages, government hospitals,night shelters, bus and railway stations

OUR WORK

EID CELEBRATIONS

Bait ul Imdaad charitable Trust celebrated Eid- ul- Adha with the poor and needy people. We ensured that those who received qurbani packets were among the most vulnerable in their communities including widows, orphans , the elderly, internally displaced persons, refugees and the poor.

OUR WORK

DRINKNG WATER

Our trust recognizes the critical importance of the issues and focuses on the most affected areas to tackle water crisis by providing people with sources of clean water.

Free drinking water facility at Nilofer hospital was started in 2018.It's still in use.

Borewell Projects:We have completed 7 (bore well)drinking water projects to date .

ZAKAT

It is an obligatory act ordained by Allah (The Glorified and Exalted) to be performed by every adult and able bodied Muslim. It is an important pillar among the five pillars of Islam. Zakat is a part of the wealth and property that Muslims must pay annually, to help the poor of their community.

One of the most important principles of Islam is that all things belong to God, and that wealth is therefore held by human beings in trust. The word Zakat means both 'purification' and 'growth'. Our possessions are purified by setting aside a proportion for those in need, and, like the pruning of plants, this cutting back balances and encourages new growth.
